The Problem

The current reporting pipeline is broken at both ends.

The FDA receives fewer than 1% of adverse drug events that occur. The bottleneck isn't awareness — it's friction.

📋

Forms designed for regulators, not people

MedWatch asks for NDC numbers, lot codes, and route of administration. Most patients can't answer these without abandoning the report entirely.

🔇

Experiences go unreported — and signals are missed

When reporting is too hard, patients give up. That means the FDA never learns about patterns that could protect the next person taking the same drug.

🧩

No bridge to the care team

Even when patients do report, there's no structured handoff to the physician. The information exists in a PDF and goes nowhere useful.

⏱️

Manual case processing is a time sink

PV teams spend hours extracting structured data from unstructured narratives, cleaning inconsistent inputs, and filling out ICSRs by hand.

🗂️

Incomplete intake means incomplete records

When patient-reported data arrives as free text or a phone call, key fields — dechallenge, concomitant medications, outcome categories — are routinely missing.

🔗

Patient-to-submission pipeline has no connective tissue

Intake, coding, narrative drafting, and E2B submission happen in disconnected systems. Every handoff is a manual re-entry risk.

How It Works

A conversation that does the regulatory work for you.

MurmurPV replaces a form with a conversation. The AI knows what the FDA needs — and asks for it in plain language, adapting to what the patient actually knows.

1

Patient starts a conversation — by voice or text

No account required. No form to fill out. The AI asks plain-language questions about what happened: which drug, what symptoms, when, for how long. If the patient doesn't know an answer, that's recorded too — not a dead end.

2

The AI structures the record in real time

While the patient talks, MurmurPV populates a structured record against the MedWatch 3500B schema — capturing concomitant medications, dechallenge timelines, and FDA outcome categories automatically. A deterministic completeness check catches any gaps and asks targeted follow-up questions before finalizing.

3

Patient reviews and approves

Before anything is exported, the patient sees a plain-language summary of every captured field and can correct anything that's wrong. They control what gets exported and where it goes.

4

Physician receives a regulation-ready record

The output is a MedWatch 3500B PDF the patient can submit directly, plus a structured handoff JSON for downstream physician processing — MedDRA coding, narrative drafting, and E2B R3 submission. No re-entry. No translation layer.

Built for High-Stakes Data

Designed like a medical system, not a consumer app.

Patient health data deserves infrastructure-level seriousness. MurmurPV is architected to meet it.

Ephemeral by default

Nothing is stored on MurmurPV's servers without explicit patient consent. The session ends; the data stays with the patient. No persistent health records without a deliberate opt-in.

Self-hostable within your VPC

For organizations with data sovereignty requirements — CROs, hospitals, trial sites — MurmurPV can run entirely within your own infrastructure. Your data never leaves your stack.

Open-source patient intake (Apache 2.0)

FDA-schema compliant output
